About the developer behind the code

I started out the way most front-end developers do — recreating buttons and layouts from screenshots just to understand why they looked the way they did. That habit turned into a Computer Science degree at NFC Institute of Engineering & Fertilizer Research, and eventually into shipping real interfaces for real people.

Today I split my time between coursework, freelance front-end work on Fiverr and Upwork, and applying for internships — including in banking and fintech, where clean, trustworthy UI actually matters. I care about the details AI tools tend to skip: semantic markup, layouts that don't break at 375px, and code a teammate can actually read.

Off-screen, I'm usually deep in networking fundamentals or digital marketing basics — I like understanding the systems a website has to survive on, not just the pixels on top.
